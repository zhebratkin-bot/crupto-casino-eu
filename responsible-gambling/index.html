Responsible Gambling

Gambling should be entertaining, not harmful. Learn how to gamble responsibly, recognize warning signs, and access support if you need help.

What is Responsible Gambling?

Responsible gambling means enjoying gambling as a form of entertainment while maintaining control over the time and money you spend. It's about making informed decisions, understanding the risks, and knowing when to stop. Gambling should never interfere with your financial obligations, relationships, or mental health.

Guidelines for Safe Gambling

  • Set a Budget: Decide how much money you can afford to lose before you start playing, and stick to it
  • Set Time Limits: Decide how long you'll play and take regular breaks
  • Never Chase Losses: Accept losses as part of the game and never try to win back what you've lost
  • Don't Gamble Under Influence: Avoid gambling when drinking alcohol or using substances
  • Keep Perspective: Remember that gambling is entertainment, not a way to make money
  • Balance with Other Activities: Ensure gambling doesn't replace other hobbies and social activities
  • Don't Borrow to Gamble: Never gamble with borrowed money or money needed for bills

Warning Signs of Problem Gambling

It's important to recognize the warning signs of problem gambling. If you notice any of these signs in yourself or someone you know, it may be time to seek help:

  • Spending more money or time gambling than you can afford
  • Feeling the need to gamble with increasing amounts of money
  • Trying repeatedly to cut back or stop gambling without success
  • Feeling restless or irritable when trying to reduce gambling
  • Gambling to escape problems or relieve negative feelings
  • Lying to family or friends about gambling activities
  • Jeopardizing relationships, jobs, or education due to gambling
  • Relying on others for money to cover gambling losses
  • Chasing losses or gambling to recover money lost
  • Feeling guilty or ashamed about gambling

Tools to Help You Stay in Control

Most reputable crypto casinos offer tools to help you maintain control over your gambling:

  • Deposit Limits: Set daily, weekly, or monthly limits on how much you can deposit
  • Loss Limits: Set maximum amounts you're willing to lose in a given period
  • Session Time Limits: Set reminders or automatic logouts after a certain time
  • Reality Checks: Receive periodic notifications showing how long you've been playing
  • Self-Exclusion: Temporarily or permanently block yourself from accessing the casino
  • Cooling-Off Periods: Take a short break from gambling (24 hours to several weeks)
